Okay, so with multiple goroutines calling your increment function concurrently, 
you're looking at a classic data race issue, which is a specific kind of race condition.
What happens is that the steps inside increment aren't a single, indivisible operation. 
Because goroutines execute asynchronously, they can step on each other's toes: one might
read the counter, then another reads the same old value before the first one writes its 
update back. This means some increments get lost, and your counter won't reach the 
expected sum.

This scenario perfectly illustrates why Go has that really important proverb: 
"Don't communicate by sharing memory, share memory by communicating.". Your current 
increment shares the counter directly in memory, which needs careful synchronization.
